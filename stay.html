    6. Gran Vía

    Gran Vía is one of the main streets in Madrid and is known for its beautiful architecture, shops, and theaters. The street is a popular spot for shopping, dining, and entertainment, and is home to several iconic buildings, including the Metropolis Building and the Telefonica Building.

  5. 7. Santiago Bernabéu Stadium

    Home to the Real Madrid football club, the Santiago Bernabéu Stadium is a must-visit for sports fans. The stadium offers guided tours that take visitors behind the scenes and give them a chance to see the trophy room, the players' tunnel, and the pitch.

    8. Mercado de San Miguel

    Mercado de San Miguel is a historic market in Madrid that is home to a variety of food stalls and vendors. The market is a great place to sample traditional Spanish dishes, such as paella, tapas, and churros, and to pick up souvenirs and gifts.

    9. Templo de Debod

    The Templo de Debod is an ancient Egyptian temple that was dismantled and rebuilt in Madrid. The temple is located in the Parque del Oeste and is a popular spot to watch the sunset and take in panoramic views of the city.

  6. 10. Churros con Chocolate

    No visit to Madrid is complete without trying churros con chocolate. This delicious treat consists of fried dough dipped in thick hot chocolate and is a popular breakfast or snack in Spain. The best place to try churros con chocolate in Madrid is at the famous Chocolatería San Ginés.
