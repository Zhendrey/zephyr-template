Top 10 reasons to visit

Paris, France

  1. 1. The Prado Museum

    One of the most important art galleries in the world, the Prado Museum is a must-see for art lovers. The museum houses an extensive collection of European art from the 12th to the early 19th century, including works by Goya, Velázquez, El Greco, and Bosch.

    The Royal Palace of Madrid is the official residence of the Spanish Royal Family. The palace is a stunning example of Baroque architecture and is one of the largest palaces in Europe. Visitors can tour the palace and see the opulent rooms, the royal chapel, and the armory.

  3. 3. Retiro Park

    Retiro Park is a beautiful green oasis in the heart of Madrid. The park is a popular spot for locals and tourists alike, who come to relax, have a picnic, or take a boat ride on the lake. The park is also home to several fountains, statues, and the Crystal Palace.

  4. 4. Plaza Mayor

    Plaza Mayor is the main square in Madrid and is a popular meeting spot for locals and tourists. The square is surrounded by beautiful buildings with arcades and is home to several cafes, restaurants, and shops. Visitors can also see the statue of King Philip III in the center of the square.

  5. 5. Puerta del Sol

    Puerta del Sol is one of the most famous squares in Madrid and is the symbolic center of the city. The square is home to several important landmarks, including the Tio Pepe sign, the statue of the Bear and the Strawberry Tree, and the clock tower of the old post office.

  6. 6. Gran Vía

    Gran Vía is one of the main streets in Madrid and is known for its beautiful architecture, shops, and theaters. The street is a popular spot for shopping, dining, and entertainment, and is home to several iconic buildings, including the Metropolis Building and the Telefonica Building.

  7. 7. Santiago Bernabéu Stadium

    Home to the Real Madrid football club, the Santiago Bernabéu Stadium is a must-visit for sports fans. The stadium offers guided tours that take visitors behind the scenes and give them a chance to see the trophy room, the players' tunnel, and the pitch.

  8. 8. Mercado de San Miguel

    Mercado de San Miguel is a historic market in Madrid that is home to a variety of food stalls and vendors. The market is a great place to sample traditional Spanish dishes, such as paella, tapas, and churros, and to pick up souvenirs and gifts.

  9. 9. Templo de Debod

    The Templo de Debod is an ancient Egyptian temple that was dismantled and rebuilt in Madrid. The temple is located in the Parque del Oeste and is a popular spot to watch the sunset and take in panoramic views of the city.

  10. 10. Churros con Chocolate

    No visit to Madrid is complete without trying churros con chocolate. This delicious treat consists of fried dough dipped in thick hot chocolate and is a popular breakfast or snack in Spain. The best place to try churros con chocolate in Madrid is at the famous Chocolatería San Ginés.
